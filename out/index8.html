The Life and Story of Prophet Muhammad

Early Life

Prophet Muhammad was born in Mecca in 570 CE. His father, Abdullah, passed away before he was born, and his mother, Aminah, died when he was six years old. He was then raised by his grandfather and, later, his uncle.

As a young man, he worked as a merchant and became known for his honesty and fairness in business.

Revelation and Prophethood

When Prophet Muhammad was 40 years old, he received his first revelation from Allah through the Angel Gabriel. This event marked the beginning of his prophethood.

He began preaching the message of Islam to the people of Mecca, but faced opposition from the city's leaders. In 622 CE, he and his followers were forced to leave Mecca and migrate to Medina. This event, known as the Hijra, marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

Spread of Islam

Prophet Muhammad continued to preach the message of Islam in Medina and gained many followers. He also established a constitution that guaranteed rights and protections for all citizens, regardless of their religion.

Over the next several years, he and his followers engaged in several battles with the Meccans, but eventually triumphed and returned to Mecca in 630 CE.

Death

Prophet Muhammad continued to lead the Muslim community until his death in 632 CE. His teachings and example continue to inspire millions of Muslims around the world today.
